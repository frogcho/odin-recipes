Frog Sourdough

Let's get this bread!

Original Recipe

Ingredients
  • 2 packages (40g each) of active, dry yeast
  • 1-1/4 cups warm water (45 - 50 degrees Celsius)
  • 1 cup sourdough starter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 6 to 6-1/2 cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • Melted butter
  • Butterknife
Instructions
  1. Ensure your sourdough starter is fed for about a week
  2. Dissolve two packets of active dry yeast in warm water
  3. Give mixture quick whisk and add the sourdough starter (this should create a foamy, rich-smelling mixture)
  4. Add in the eggs, sugar, oil, salt and three cups of flour and mix until smooth (stand mixer with dough hook makes this step much easier!)
  5. Add in the remaining flour to make a dough
  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic (you can do this by hand or continue to use your stand mixer)
  7. Move the dough to a greased bowl and let it rise in a warm place for about an hour
  8. Deflate the dough, lightly flour a surface (your kitchen bench works great!) and turn the dough onto it
  9. Start with a larger chunk of dough and shape it into an oval to form the frog's body
  10. Add two back legs and two front legs (along with feet) using smaller pieces of dough
  11. Roll two small balls and stick them on top of your “frog” to create the eyes
  12. Don't forget to carve a smile with a butterknife!
  13. Brush the frog bread with an egg wash, then bake it at 180 degrees celsius for about 45 minutes or until golden brown
  14. Eat bread!
